# CS-255-System-Analysis-and-Design

## DriverPass Project Summary
The DriverPass project focused on designing a system for a client who wanted to improve how students prepare for their driving tests.
The client needed a system that would allow students to purchase packages, schedule driving lessons, take practice tests, and track their progress.

## What I Did Well
I fell like my UML diagrams all came out well. They all showed enough information to get the point of the diagrams across without having so much that they were confusing and overloaded.

## What I Would Revide
I would go back over the functional and nonfunctional requirements. I still have a hard time putting the requirements into the correct category. 
I would improve on it but breaking them down more in my head and determine if the requirements have inputs, outputs or preformed a function, or if they were more of the system operation.

## Understanding and Implementing User Needs
I used the interview transcript to help determine the user’s needs and I made sure to put those needs into the system design. 
You have to consider the user’s needs because you are making the system for them and if it doesn’t solve the user’s problems, there is not point in it, even if it is well built.

## My Approach to Software Design
When designing software, I approach it by breaking the system into smaller components and understanding how each part interacts with the others. 
As a person with a developer background, I really understood the class diagrams and creating them helped me visualize the system from different angles. 
In the future, I plan to continue using structured analysis, iterative refinement, and user centered design strategies to ensure that the systems I design are both functional and easy to use.
